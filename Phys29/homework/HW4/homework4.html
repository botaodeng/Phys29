Problem 1: Benford's Law

Benford's law refers to the observation that in many real-life datasets, the frequency of the first digits of each number is not uniform. If the digits were distributed uniformly, they would each occur as the first digit with equal probability, i.e. 1/9 per digit, or 11.1% of the time. Instead, there tend to be many more numbers with the first significant (decimal) digit of 1 as compared to 9. This applies best to datsets where the numbers span several orders of magnitude. Familiarize yourself with Benford's law by reading the Wikipedia page here. Incidentally, Benfords law is used in forensic accounting to detect fraud, see here.

It lists the number of inhabitants for every town in the USA.
